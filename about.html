Hello! I am currently a senior at Middlebury College studying computer science and geography (expected graduation in Winter 2021). I am currently a research assistant for Professor Jeff Howarth working on developing Google Earth Engine applications for justice-oriented applications and tutorials for introductory Environmental GIS courses. This past summer, I worked with Professor Howarth on applying machine learning approaches to identify land cover change on the Channel Islands of California. I also work with an interdisciplinary research group under Professor Carly Thomsen studying crisis pregnancy centers and abortion access in the United States.

During summer 2019, I was the graphics and data visualization intern at the Los Angeles Times. Previously, I completed a fellowship with the Voting Rights Data Institute and interned with the GIS/graphics team at the Pioneer Valley Planning Commission.

At Middlebury, I've also worked as a research assistant for Professor Guntram Herb studying Indigenous experiences with U.S.-Canada border, as a teaching assistant in introductory environmental and human geography-based GIS courses and computer science courses, and as an advanced computer consultant at the College's technology helpdesk.
