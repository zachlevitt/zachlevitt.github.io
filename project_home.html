Designing Interactive Maps for Climate Change

BY ZACH LEVITT

Given the complexity, urgency and inherent spatial nature of climate change, maps have become a common communication technique for environmental activists and researchers. Yet while innovations in cartography have allowed these maps to proliferate across digital contexts, their effectiveness is often limited by poor design choices. This project offers a reference guide for designing interactive thematic web maps and an example map related to climate change that highlights essential design decision points. The guide is informed by a literature review of published scholarship on interactive cartographic design and an analysis of existing web-based interactive maps related to climate change.Learn more about this project
